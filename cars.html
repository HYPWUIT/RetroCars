Excalibur (1965)

A neo-classic automobile styled after the 1928 Mercedes-Benz SSK. Built in the United States, Excalibur cars combined vintage luxury design with modern V8 engines.

Panther J72 (1972)

Inspired by the Jaguar SS100, this British neo-classic roadster featured elegant pre-war styling with modern mechanical components.

MP Lafer (1974)

A Brazilian-built roadster inspired by the MG TD. It combined fiberglass bodywork with Volkswagen mechanical parts.

Panther De Ville (1974)

A luxury neo-classic car inspired by 1930s Bugatti styling. It featured exaggerated fenders and vintage proportions.

Panther FF (1975)

A retro-styled British sports car featuring classic long-hood proportions with modern engineering underneath.

Panther Lima (1976)

A smaller roadster model from Panther, inspired by traditional British sports cars of the 1950s and 60s.

Cadillac Seville (1980)

This generation featured distinctive “bustle-back” styling reminiscent of 1930s luxury sedans.

Phillips Berlina (1980)

A limited-production neo-classic vehicle styled after the Mercedes-Benz 540K.

Imperial (1981)

The Chrysler Imperial of the early 1980s adopted retro-inspired bustle-back rear styling.

Lincoln Continental (1982)

Featured similar bustle-back styling cues, evoking pre-war American luxury cars.

Panther Kallista (1982)

A retro roadster inspired by traditional British sports cars, offering lightweight construction and modern engines.

Evante (1984)

Inspired by the Lotus Elan, this British sports car offered retro design combined with modern performance upgrades.

Eniak Antique (1984)

An Italian neo-classic roadster blending vintage styling with contemporary mechanical components.

Nissan Be-1 (1987)

A small retro-styled city car designed with rounded, minimalist 1950s-inspired aesthetics.

Nissan Pao (1989)

Designed to resemble classic European small cars, with simple lines and nostalgic detailing.

Nissan S-Cargo (1989)

A quirky retro van inspired by the 1948 Citroën 2CV and French postal vans.

Nissan Figaro (1991)

A limited-production convertible evoking 1960s European styling with pastel colors and chrome accents.

Subaru Vivio Bistro (1992)

A retro-themed kei car based on the Subaru Vivio, styled to resemble classic European microcars.
